A Smart White Cane Design Integrated with IoT Application (Published in The Journal of Health Sciences)

Objective

Statistics show that 56,039 people in Taiwan are visually impaired. People with visual impairment often experience numerous problems and difficulties during activities of daily living, both indoors and outdoors. This study was conducted to integrate Internet of Things (IoT) sensors into a white cane to design a smart white cane that can aid people with visual impairment. Subsequently, the smart white cane was combined with a mobile app program to construct a convenient IoT system for aiding the everyday activities of visually impaired people.

Methods

At the sensing end, an Arduino board integrated various sensors into a white cane. These sensors included an obstacle detection sensor for identifying obstacles ahead, an ambient light sensor that activates in low-light conditions to alert pedestrians, and an emergency response sensor.

Concurrently, a mobile app was designed to offer location records, bus stop announcements, and near-field communication shortcut applications, among other functions. Additionally, a website application program was developed to provide inquiry and management functions for administrators and family members.

Results

A comprehensive IoT system was developed to assist visually impaired individuals in their daily activities. Sensors were integrated into a white cane for obstacle detection and light sensing. A mobile app program was designed to interface with these sensors for warning notifications and connect to an open database for real-time bus arrival queries and incident reporting. Near-field communication shortcuts were implemented to provide scheduled notifications, and all data can be seamlessly synchronized with a back-end database as required. Additionally, a web application program was created to provide administrators and family members with access to pertinent information.

Conclusions

This study encompassed the development of a comprehensive IoT system that integrates a mobile app with a smart white cane. The system offers five primary functions, including emergency number dialing, location information provision, obstacle avoidance and distance measurement, timer notifications, and bus stop announcements. The IoT system was further integrated with websites to provide detailed information about its functionalities and user information management features. The resulting system has the potential to enhance the safety and convenience of visually impaired individuals, ultimately improving their quality of life.
